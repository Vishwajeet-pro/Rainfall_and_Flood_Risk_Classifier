Risk Factors by Category

Flood risk is influenced by multiple categories of factors. Understanding each helps explain why a region is at high or low risk.

Climate & Weather

  • monsoonIntensity: Strength of monsoon rainfall
  • ClimateChange: Long-term climate patterns

Land Use & Vegetation

  • Deforestation: Loss of forest cover
  • Urbanization: Development of urban areas
  • WetlandLoss: Loss of natural water retention areas
  • AgriculturalPractices: Farming methods affecting drainage

Infrastructure

  • DamsQuality: Condition of water control structures
  • deteriorating infrastructure: Aging drainage systems
  • Siltation: Sediment buildup in waterways
  • DrainageSystems: Effectiveness of drainage

Human & Natural Hazards

  • Encroachments: Building in flood-prone areas
  • InadequatePlanning: Poor urban planning
  • ineffectiveDisasterPreparedness: Lack of disaster readiness
  • Landslides: Slope failures triggering floods
  • Coastal vulnerability: Risk in coastal areas

Key Insights

🔝 Top Driver: Deteriorating infrastructure (5.88%) — aging drainage systems and water control structures are the strongest predictors of flood risk.

⚠️ Disaster Readiness Matters: Ineffective disaster preparedness (5.71%) shows that regions without strong warning and response systems face higher flood risk.

🏗️ Urban Growth Impact: Urbanization (5.53%) and Siltation (5.69%) are in the top 5, indicating that rapid city development without proper water management significantly increases risk.

📊 Balanced Assessment: All 20 factors contribute meaningfully (3.7%-5.9% importance), showing that flood risk is a complex phenomenon requiring multi-faceted solutions.
